Chicken Satay Recipe

Description

Chicken satay is a traditional Indonesian dish consisting of pieces of chicken skewered on bamboo sticks, grilled over charcoal, and served with a sweet and savory peanut sauce or soy sauce-based sauce. This legendary dish has its roots in Middle Eastern culinary traditions (such as kebabs) brought to Java by Muslim traders centuries ago, which were then adapted to incorporate local spices. Today, chicken satay is one of Indonesia's most popular national dishes, often served with slices of lontong or warm rice, topped with fried shallots and fresh pickles.

Ingredients

  • 250 grams chicken, diced
  • Lime juice to taste
  • 500 ml clean water

Marinade

  • 1 kg peanuts
  • 250 grams palm sugar
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 6 shallots
  • Kaffir lime leaves to taste
  • 250 grams Bango sweet soy sauce
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil as needed
  • Black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Rub the chicken pieces with lime juice and a pinch of salt. Let sit for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to soak in.
  2. Fry the peanuts until golden brown, then grind them into a paste using a blender or mortar and pestle.
  3. Sauté the minced shallots and garlic in a little cooking oil until fragrant.
  4. Add the kaffir lime leaves, ground peanuts, palm sugar, Bango sweet soy sauce, salt, pepper, and water. Stir well and cook until the sauce thickens.
  5. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning to your liking.
  6. Skewer the marinated chicken pieces onto satay skewers.
  7. Brush the cooked peanut sauce evenly over the chicken.
  8. Grill the satay over hot coals or in a grill pan, basting continuously with the remaining peanut sauce until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has been absorbed.
