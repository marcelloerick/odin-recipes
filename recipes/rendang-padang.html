Rendang Padang Recipe

Home

Description

Rendang is a traditional Indonesian dish originating from the Padang region of West Sumatra. It is made with beef that is slow-cooked with coconut milk and a blend of spices until it becomes tender and flavorful.

Cooking beef rendang is a tradition observed during Eid al-Fitr. Although the cooking process takes quite a long time, the resulting flavor will not disappoint.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef, cut into pieces
  • 1 L coconut milk
  • 500 mL full cream milk
  • 3 lemongrass stalks, bruised
  • 5 cm cinnamon stick
  • 1 nutmeg
  • 1.5 pieces of dried yellow mangosteen
  • 5 cloves
  • 5 cardamom pods
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 turmeric leaf, tied in a knot
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 8 kaffir lime leaves

Ground Spices

  • 100 g curly chili peppers
  • 4 red chili peppers, seeded
  • 10 cloves of garlic
  • 20 shallots
  • 2 finger-lengths of ginger
  • 2 finger-lengths of galangal
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, white parts only
  • 4 cm cinnamon stick
  • 2 tbsp salt
  • 1 tsp seasoning

Instructions

  1. Boil the beef until it's cooked through and tender. You can also use a pressure cooker to speed up the cooking process.
  2. Next, blend the rendang spice paste in a blender to make it smoother.
  3. Sauté the blended spice paste in a pot or skillet over low heat until fragrant.
  4. Add lemongrass, cinnamon, nutmeg, dried yellow mangosteen, cloves, cardamom, star anise, turmeric leaves, bay leaves, and kaffir lime leaves.
  5. Add coconut milk and full cream milk.
  6. Cook until the coconut milk thickens and becomes oily.
  7. Add the beef, which has been cooked until tender and cut into pieces to your liking.
  8. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the coconut milk sauce thickens or reduces.
  9. Taste the Padang Rendang, then remove from heat and serve.
